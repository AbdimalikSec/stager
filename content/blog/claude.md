## Why This Post Exists

If you're a final year student in Somalia and most of East and Central Africa, you've probably noticed that Claude AI isn't directly accessible from your country. You open the website and it either doesn't load or tells you the service isn't available in your region.

Meanwhile students in Europe and North America are using it to build systems, write documentation, humanize their dissertations, and essentially have a senior software engineer and academic editor available at any hour — for free. That gap is real and it's unfair.

This post is for students who want to close that gap. I did this myself for my final year project and dissertation. Everything here is something I actually used.



## Why Claude Over ChatGPT

ChatGPT is the most well-known AI tool and most students pay $20 a month for the Plus plan to get access to GPT-4. Claude is equivalent in quality — in some areas better — and the free tier is genuinely usable for serious work.

Claude writes longer, more structured documents without losing coherence halfway through. It follows complex instructions precisely — when you tell it "write this in APA 7th format, Times New Roman 12pt, double spaced, with a 4cm left margin," it does all of those things at once. It handles technical documentation, code, and academic writing in the same conversation. For building a system and writing a dissertation simultaneously, it's the better tool and it costs nothing.


## Step 1 — Get a VPN

Claude's regional restriction works at the IP level. Your device has an IP address that tells websites which country you're in. A VPN replaces that IP with one from a different country. Claude sees a US or European IP and the restriction disappears.

**Windscribe** is the best free option. It gives you 10GB per month on the free tier — enough for regular Claude usage. Sign up at windscribe.com, download the app, and connect to a US or UK server.

**ProtonVPN** free tier has no data limit but runs slower. It works, just takes more patience.

Once the VPN is connected, go to claude.ai. It loads normally.



## Step 2 — Create Your Account

Creating a Claude account needs an email address and sometimes a phone number for verification.

The email is straightforward — any Gmail works.

For the phone number: Claude may ask you to verify with a number from a supported country. If your local number doesn't work, ask a family member, friend, or classmate who lives in or has a number registered in an unrestricted country — UK, US, Kenya, UAE, and most European countries all work. Ask them to receive one verification SMS for you. You enter their number, they read you the code, you enter it, verification is done. Your account is yours from that point — that number is never asked for again during normal use.

One message, done.



## Step 3 — How to Use Claude for Your Final Year Project

### Building Your System

Tell Claude the full picture upfront:

```
I'm building a [describe your system] using [your tech stack].
My database is [MySQL/MongoDB/etc].
My backend is [Node.js/Flask/etc].
My frontend is [React/HTML/etc].
Help me build [specific feature].
```

The more context you give, the better the output. Claude remembers everything in the conversation, so you can say "now add authentication to what we just built" and it knows exactly what you mean. Work through your entire system feature by feature in one long conversation.

### Writing Your Dissertation Chapters

For each chapter, give Claude your university's exact formatting requirements and what you want covered:

```
Write Chapter 2 (Literature Review) for my dissertation on [your topic].
Format: APA 7th edition, Times New Roman 12pt, double spaced, justified.
Word count: approximately 3000 words.
Cover these themes: [list your themes].
Cite at least 15 academic sources published after 2018.
```

Claude will write a structured draft. Read it, edit it to add your own voice and specific details from your actual research, then use it as your base. Never submit AI output without reading and editing it — your supervisor will ask you questions about every section and you need to know what's in there.

### Documentation

For system documentation, API docs, and user manuals:

```
Write technical documentation for this function: [paste your code]
Write a user manual section explaining how to [feature]
Generate API documentation for these endpoints: [paste your routes]
```



## Step 4 — Humanizing Your Writing With a Claude Skill

This is one of the most useful things for students. AI-generated text has patterns that plagiarism and AI detection tools recognize. Turnitin, Scribbr, and similar tools flag it. You need to remove those patterns before submitting.

Claude has a skills system. Skills are instruction files that teach Claude how to do a specific task in a specific way. The humanizer skill was built specifically to remove AI writing patterns — inflated language, repetitive sentence structure, excessive hedging, promotional tone — and rewrite text so it sounds like a real person wrote it.

### How to Get and Install the Humanizer Skill

Go to **skills.sh** — a website that hosts hundreds of community-built Claude skills. Search for "humanizer" and download the skill file to your computer.

To add it to Claude:

1. Open Claude at claude.ai
2. Go to your profile settings
3. Find the Skills section
4. Upload the humanizer skill file you downloaded


That's the setup done. Now every time you want to write a chapter, open Claude and say:

**Write me this Chapter and use humanizer and give formats and all things
you want it to Follow**

Claude rewrites it. Paste the result into Scribbr's free AI detection checker before submitting. Done.



## What to Do When the Free VPN Runs Out

Windscribe's 10GB free tier resets monthly. If you run out:

**Windscribe trick:** create a second free account with a different email address. Another 10GB.

**Alternatives that don't need Claude:**

For quick questions, essay drafts, and code help — **Microsoft Copilot** at copilot.microsoft.com is free, has no regional restrictions, requires no account, and runs on GPT-4. It doesn't write as long or as precisely as Claude but it works without any setup at all.

**Google Gemini** at gemini.google.com is free and available in most African countries without a VPN. Good for research summaries and shorter writing tasks.

Use Claude for your most important work — dissertation chapters, complex system features, long documentation. Use Copilot or Gemini for quick questions. That way your Windscribe allocation lasts the full month.
